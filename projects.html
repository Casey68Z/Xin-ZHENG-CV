Projects

Research on Digital Inclusion of Chinese Technology Enterprises | NGO Green Lights Year (2022-2023)

  • Led a 30-person team of undergraduates to complete an independent report.
  • Investigated the digital inclusion status of Chinese technology companies through desktop research, literature reviews, and corporate interviews, and explored practices and potential regulatory policies to promote digital inclusion.
  • Produced the first systematic research report on digital inclusion performance and cutting-edge methods of Chinese technology companies, which was published in both Chinese and English and announced at COP27 in collaboration with the World Benchmark Alliance.

Research on Development of Listed Corporations in Advanced Areas | Think Tank of University (2021)

  • Collected, organized, counted, and analyzed the data and information of listed companies in developed regions of China through the Wind database using SPSS for quantitative data analysis.
  • Studied the relationship between the development status of listed companies in the four advanced economic development regions of Shanghai, Beijing, Shenzhen, and Hangzhou and the government’s policy support for listed companies; analyzed and obtained policy advice and helped bolster Suzhou’s development of enterprises in Suzhou Industrial Park.
  • Independently wrote five research reports and co-wrote a political paper of over 100,000 words on high-quality company development policy recommendations for the Suzhou Industrial Park government.

Conference Presentations

  • “Water Right Trading and Regional Development: Quantitative Evidence from China”
    With Tianshu Li. Presentation at The 10th International Workshop on Regional, Urban, and Spatial Economics in China, Beijing, August 2023.
  • “Post-Pandemic Surge in Food Away From Home: Economic Validation and Sustainability Impacts on Water and Energy”
    With Jing Yi. Accepted for presentation at Agricultural and Applied Economics Association Annual 2025, Denver, July 2025.
  • “Removing the Bottleneck of Water in Industrial Development: Evidence from Regional Water Right Transactions in China”
    with Tianshu Li and Wanqian Luo. Accepted for presentation at the 2025 North America Annual Conference of the Chinese Economists Society, Ann Arbor, March 2025.
    Using quasi-experimental methods and county-level data, this study investigates the economic impact of water trading recorded by the China Water Exchange since 2016. It highlights how efficient water allocation drives GDP growth and industrial upgrading in buyer regions, while seller regions exhibit limited economic development.

Work in Progress

  • “The Suppression and Rebound of Dining-Out Demand: Empirical Evidence from U.S. COVID-19.”
    This paper uses a Difference-in-Differences model, event study, and machine learning techniques to examine the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on food-away-from-home demand across the United States. By analyzing data from 2010 to 2023 and using stay-at-home policies as policy event nodes, the study explores post-pandemic demand growth and assesses the environmental implications of these changes.
